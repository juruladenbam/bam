Biography

Raden Saleh Sjarif Boestaman was a pioneering Indonesian Romantic painter of Arab-Javanese ethnicity. He was considered to be the first "modern" artist from Indonesia (then the Dutch East Indies), and his paintings corresponded with nineteenth-century romanticism which was popular in Europe at the time. He also expressed his cultural roots and grievances against Dutch colonial rule in his work.

Saleh was born into a noble regent family in Semarang. His extraordinary talent was recognized early, leading to his education in Europe where he spent over 20 years. Upon his return, he served as a conservator for the colonial government's collection of art.

Cultural Note

The title Raden indicates nobility in Javanese culture, typically descendants of kings or regents. Mbah is an honorific for an elder or ancestor, showing deep respect.
